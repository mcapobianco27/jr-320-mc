Milan Cortina: The Hosts of the 25th Winter Olympic Games

By Michael Capobianco

The 2026 Olympic Winter Games are set to kick off on Friday evening with the opening ceremony at the San Siro Stadium in Milan, Italy. The 25th rendition of the legendary event will take place across Northern Italy, with Milan and the village of Cortina d'Ampezzo in the Italian Dolomites mountain range serving as the two primary hosts.

The map below shows where the different Olympic events will take place.

As the area prepares to welcome the world's best athletes and their fans, some of the construction is coming down to the wire.

The Milan Cortina games mark the fourth time Italy has hosted the Olympics. Two decades ago, Turin held that honor, and 70 years ago in 1956, the Games were hosted in Cortina D'Ampezzo. For the next few weeks, the sporting world will be watching the events unfold in Northern Italy once again.
